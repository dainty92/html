Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup Recipe

Pounded yam is a Nigerian food native to the Yoruba, Igbo, Ebira and Tiv ethnic groups. It is a traditional food called Iyan in Yoruba. It is mostly prepared by pounding boiled yam with a mortar and pestle. Pounded yam is similar to mashed potatoes but heavier. It is a smooth and tasty delicacy traditionally eaten with the hands. Iyán is consumed in Ondo State, Ijesha, Edo, Benue and Ekiti in Nigeria, among others. It can be served with egusi, soup, jute leaves soup (ewedu), stewed spinach (efo riro) or okra soup.

Egusi soup is a popular soup recipe in the Western part of Africa. The egusi soup is often thickened with ground melon seeds. This soup is best served with pounded yam, fufu or eba. Egusi soup and pounded yam is such a mouthwatering and appetising dish to behold.

Ingredients

Ingredients Quantity
Yam 1 tuber
Egusi/melon seed 2 cup
Frozen Spinach thawed 340g
Fresh Tomato 1
Fresh Pepper 3
Stock Cube 2
Palm Oil 1 cup
Boneless Chicken 1 Pound
Sliced Onion 1 Medium sized
Water 2 cups
Dried Shrimp To taste
Dried fish To taste
Salt To taste
Crayfish To taste

Steps/Direction

Pounded Yam

Method 1

Using Mortar and Pestle

  1. Use a knife to peel the yam. Get rid of any discolorations or spots.
  2. Pour into the water and wash off all dirt immediately after cutting into large chunks.
  3. Cook the yams under high heat with enough water to cover them.
  4. Boil for approximately 20 minutes until tender. Drain yams thoroughly in a colander immediately after removing them from the heat.
  5. Clean the mortar and pestle by washing them. Pound yam until smooth and stretchy in a mortar.
  6. Add water when necessary while pounding
  7. Roll the balls and wrap them in plastic.
Method 2

Using Blender

  1. Cut yam into smaller pieces and boiled for about 20 minutes until soft.
  2. A food processor or blender can be used to process cooked yams. Process for 20-30 seconds, then pulse to check if they’re smooth.
  3. To stretch and soften, add a couple of tablespoons of water at a time.
  4. Clean the mortar and pestle by washing them. Pound yam until smooth and stretchy in a mortar.
  5. Add water when necessary while pounding
  6. Roll the balls, shaped into any size and wrap them in plastic.

Egusi Soup

  1. Place chicken and dried fish in a pot after it has been rinsed.
  2. Add 1 onion, 2 Maggi cubes, and salt to taste.
  3. Add one and a half cups of water to a boil on high heat for 25 minutes. Turn the heat down and simmer for 10 more minutes.
  4. Using a blender, puree the fresh pepper, tomatoes and remaining onion. Place onion pepper mixture in a bowl and set aside.
  5. To make melon seed powder, place shrimp, crayfish, and melon seeds in a blender. Pulse until powder forms. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  6. Set the spinach aside after squeezing out the excess liquid.
  7. Slightly heat up red oil in a saucepan, stir in onions then add grinded melon seeds.
  8. Stir in chicken, onion pepper mixture and continue cooking for 20 more minutes and stir in the spinach.
  9. For a final 5 minutes, cover and cook.
